Side-by-side comparison. Scroll down the page as the images are loading

In this page we are comparing different image formats. User/viewer test, see quality and load time. 1. GIF 2. JPG 3. PNG 4. WebP




Below you can see the list of photos and their size. List is shown in png (95kb) and in WebP (32kb) formats (again for comparison).



The photos seem to be of similar quality. Looking at the images in the browser it is difficult to tell of quality differences. Can you spot any differences?
The 2 tables above are of the same image the first is .png and the second .WebP, the content may be difficult to read, you can try zooming into the browser, to find any quality differences. By the way, notice that the 6th image here has been turned 180 degrees (inspect to see).
You can see that gif and png types are a lot larger and take longer to load (try refreshing the page). jpg and WebP load much faster. From this quick test we can observe that WebP and jpg are better for load speed.
Speed differences will be more noticeable when using slower connections, try viewing in a mobile phone with a slow connection.
Update 23 May 19 - I created a React Web App and used WebP images, but found issues when viewing images in IE and Mozilla Firefox browsers. jpg did not have such compatibility issues.

Note that this is just a quick observation and further and analysis should be taken to arrive to obtain more valid conclusions.
